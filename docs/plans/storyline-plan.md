# The Agaricalis Saga — Main Storyline Plan

## Overview

A multi-act main storyline woven into the existing mission and NPC systems. Players progress through the story by completing story missions, talking to key NPCs, and reaching faction/level thresholds. The story unfolds naturally alongside freeform gameplay — it never blocks the player, but rewards engagement with exclusive loot, lore, and gameplay unlocks.

**Core narrative**: Centuries after Captain Elenion united the Calvatian Galaxy against the Umbral Dominion — the remnants of an ancient civilization that manipulated galactic events from the shadows — new signals from the deep sectors suggest the Dominion was not destroyed, only driven underground. Their ancient technologies stir. Their agents move unseen. The fragile peace Elenion built is fracturing, and a new generation must uncover what the Precursors left behind before the Dominion rises again.

**Canonical source**: "The Agaricalis Saga: Journey Through the Cosmic Horizon" by Dan Pittman (published). The game takes place generations after the book's events, with the book's characters as legendary historical figures and the book's conflicts as the foundational lore.

## Canonical Lore Foundation (from The Agaricalis Saga)

### Book → Game Timeline

The game is set **centuries after** the events of the book. The book's story is ancient history — told through data logs, NPC dialogue, Precursor ruins, and artifact inscriptions. Players discover the book's events as lore fragments throughout the storyline.

### Key Lore Concepts

- **The Star Seeker**: Elenion's legendary ship. Built on Agaricalis, it became a "floating embassy" and symbol of unity. The Precursor Scout ship players can earn is described as built from recovered Star Seeker blueprints.
- **Cyrillium**: Energy source discovered during first contact with the Vedic. Powers most modern technology in the Calvatian Galaxy. Referenced in crafting, trade, and ship systems.
- **The Umbral Dominion** (book: "the ancient civilization" / "the hidden adversary"): Remnants of a civilization that once ruled the galaxy with unparalleled knowledge and power. They faded into myth but survived in the shadows, manipulating galactic events for centuries. Their goal: reclaim dominion over the galaxy. Elenion defeated them at the galactic core and offered integration rather than destruction — but not all accepted.
- **The Galactic Council**: Founded by Elenion post-war as a cooperative governance body. In-game, it has since fractured into the five factions players interact with. The original Council is referenced as a golden age lost to faction infighting.
- **The Research Consortium**: Founded by Miraen to study ancient artifacts collaboratively. In-game, this evolved into the Cosmic Scholars faction.
- **The Galactic Core**: Where the Dominion was based and the final battle took place. Now a forbidden zone — sealed sectors that no one enters. The story drives players toward it.
- **The Memorial**: A monument honoring those who fell in the Dominion War. Located at a specific Star Mall that serves as a story hub.
- **Ancient Artifacts/Relics**: Contain forgotten technologies and "echoes of a long-forgotten epoch." In the book, Miraen discovered they held keys to countering the Dominion. In-game, these are the fragments players collect in Act II.

## Design Principles

1. **Story never blocks gameplay** — Players can trade, fight, and explore freely. Story missions appear as optional objectives that become available at level/reputation thresholds.
2. **Interstitials create moments** — Act transitions, major reveals, and story milestones use fullscreen event overlays (the "celebration interstitial" pattern from level-up). These create memorable punctuation marks in the experience.
3. **Idle nudges keep players engaged** — Toast notifications surface when a player has been idle or grinding without story progress: "The Oracle has sent a transmission...", "Strange signals detected near your sector..."
4. **NPCs drive the narrative** — Key NPCs (The Oracle, The Hermit, Lyra Starwind, Commander Thane) serve as story anchors. Players must find them and build reputation to unlock deeper lore.
5. **Faction reputation gates content** — Each act requires minimum standing with certain factions, naturally encouraging diverse gameplay.
6. **Prizes are meaningful** — Act completion rewards include exclusive ships, items, titles, and permanent stat bonuses that can't be obtained any other way.

## Act Structure

### Prologue: Arrival (Levels 1-3)

**Theme**: You're a nobody who just arrived in the Calvatian Galaxy.

**Trigger**: Completing the tutorial.

**Story beats**:

- Tutorial ends with the player arriving at their first Star Mall — near the Memorial of the Dominion War, a monument Elenion himself commissioned
- A bartender NPC (Vex Ironhide or Mira Dustwalker, depending on starting sector) drops a hint: _"You picked a hell of a time to show up, greenhorn. Strange signals coming from the outer sectors — same kind Elenion chased before the War. The old-timers are spooked."_
- Player walks past the Memorial — brief lore pop-up about the Dominion War and Captain Elenion's coalition
- Player receives their first story mission: **"Prove Your Worth"** — complete 3 basic missions (trade, scan, visit sectors) to establish credibility
- On completion, the bartender introduces the player to their first key NPC contact

**Interstitial**: None (tutorial flow handles this)

**Rewards**:

- 500 credits bonus
- Title: "Greenhorn"
- Unlocks Act I story missions

**Story missions** (5 missions, sequential):

1. **"Signal in the Static"** — A Frontier Ranger contact asks you to investigate strange signals in 3 remote sectors. You find ancient data crystals at each location — etched with the same glyphs Miraen catalogued centuries ago.
2. **"The Scholar's Interest"** — Deliver the data crystals to a Cosmic Scholars NPC (the faction that evolved from Miraen's research consortium). They partially decode them: references to "The Convergence" and coordinates to a hidden sector. One crystal contains a fragment of Miraen's original research notes.
3. **"Into the Unknown"** — Navigate to the hidden sector (requires exploring a specific path through uncharted space). Discover a Precursor ruin — an ancient relay station, still faintly powered. Inside, find a holographic data log from Caelum describing how he built the relay network using Precursor technology he'd reverse-engineered.
4. **"The Oracle's Call"** — The relay activates and broadcasts on all frequencies. The Oracle (Cosmic Scholars faction leader, who claims spiritual descent from Miraen's consortium) contacts you directly: _"You've found one of the old places. Elenion's crew built these to watch the sealed sectors. Come to me. There is much to discuss."_
5. **"Audience with the Oracle"** — Travel to The Oracle's location. First major lore dump: The Oracle reveals the history — Elenion's coalition defeated the Umbral Dominion at the galactic core centuries ago, but Elenion offered integration, not annihilation. Most Dominion remnants accepted. Some did not. The relay network was built to watch for their return. Most relays went dark decades ago. The Oracle believes someone — or something — has been disabling them.

### Act II: The Scattered Keys (Levels 8-15)

**Theme**: The Oracle reveals that Elenion's crew hid four fragments of a master key — the same ancient relics that Miraen studied during the Dominion War — across the galaxy, one in each race's ancestral territory. They were insurance: if the Dominion ever returned, the key could reactivate the Precursor network at the galactic core. Just as Elenion once sought the artifacts in Chapter 4 of his saga, the player must now retrace that journey.

**Trigger**: Complete Act I + reach Level 8.

**Story missions** (4 parallel faction questlines + 2 connective missions = 10 total):

**Muscarian Fragment** (requires Liked+ with Traders Guild or Frontier Rangers):

1. **"Spore Network"** — A Muscarian elder reveals the fragment is hidden in the Mycelial Depths, a sector cluster their ancestors sealed off. Navigate through spore-infested sectors (environmental hazard: energy drain per turn).
2. **"The First Guardian"** — Defeat or bypass a Precursor automated defense system guarding the fragment. First major combat challenge.

**Vedic Fragment** (requires Liked+ with Cosmic Scholars): 3. **"Psionic Resonance"** — A Vedic sage teaches you to read Precursor glyphs. Follow a trail of glyph-marked sectors to find the Vedic fragment hidden inside a crystal cavern on a frozen planet. 4. **"The Mind Lock"** — The fragment is sealed behind a puzzle (in-game: a sequence of correct sector visits based on glyph clues). Requires careful exploration and note-taking.

**Kalin Fragment** (requires Accepted+ with any faction — Kalin are suspicious of everyone): 5. **"Stone and Steel"** — A Kalin warlord has the fragment but doesn't know what it is. He uses it as a trophy. Challenge: earn his respect through combat missions or trade him something of equal value. 6. **"The Kalin's Price"** — Whether by combat or barter, acquire the fragment. The warlord warns: _"The old ones feared what lies beyond. Perhaps you should too."_

**Tar'ri Fragment** (requires Liked+ with Shadow Syndicate or Traders Guild): 7. **"The Merchant's Secret"** — A Tar'ri information broker knows where the last fragment is but wants payment — not in credits, but in favors. Complete 2 smuggling/trade missions. 8. **"The Hidden Vault"** — The fragment is in the Shadow Syndicate's forbidden vault. Getting access requires either high Syndicate reputation or a daring heist mission.

**Connective missions**: 9. **"Pieces of the Puzzle"** — Deliver all 4 fragments to The Oracle. She assembles them into the Convergence Key. Major lore reveal: the key opens a path to the Convergence Point — the galactic core where Elenion fought the final battle of the Dominion War. The sealed sectors will open. What's been growing in there for centuries is unknown. 10. **"The Hermit's Warning"** — The Hermit (a reclusive NPC in an isolated sector) contacts you. He's been watching. He reveals: _"Elenion was wise, but he was merciful. He offered the Dominion integration. Most accepted. But the ones who refused — they retreated deeper into the core. They've had centuries to rebuild. And they've been disabling the relays one by one."_

### Act III: The Convergence (Levels 15-22)

**Theme**: The sealed sectors are opening. The Umbral Dominion — the faction of the ancient civilization that refused Elenion's peace — has been rebuilding in the galactic core for centuries. Their agents are disabling relay stations and silencing outposts, echoing the Tar'ri outpost attack from Elenion's era. History is repeating, and the player must do what Elenion did: build a coalition and confront the darkness at the core.

**Trigger**: Complete Act II + reach Level 15.

**Act III Opening Interstitial**:

```

═══════════════════════════════════════════

Outposts go dark — just like before the War.

The Umbral Dominion stirs.
Elenion's mercy bought centuries of peace.
That peace is ending.

The galactic core holds the answer —
if you can reach it alive.
═══════════════════════════════════════════
```

**Story missions** (8 missions):

1. **"Dark Sectors"** — Investigate 3 sectors that have gone "dark" (NPCs and outposts suddenly abandoned). Find evidence of the Dominion's return — hull fragments of ancient design, weapons signatures that match Dominion War records, scorch marks from technologies that predate the four races. The Oracle whispers: _"This is how it started for Elenion — with a Tar'ri outpost in flames."_
2. **"The Gathering Storm"** — The Oracle calls an emergency meeting of faction leaders at the Memorial Star Mall. Travel there for a cutscene: the factions argue, old grievances surface (the Galactic Council Elenion built has fractured into these very factions). But they agree to share intelligence. You're appointed as the expeditionary scout — the role Elenion himself once held.
3. **"Into the Deep"** — Navigate a gauntlet of increasingly dangerous sectors toward the galactic core. Environmental hazards, Precursor defense systems reactivating, and the first encounters with Dominion Shade vessels — ancient ships running on technologies far beyond anything the four races possess.
4. **"First Contact: The Shades"** — Combat encounter with a Dominion Shade patrol. These are tough — their ships use ancient energy dispersion tech (the same kind Caelum once reverse-engineered for the Star Seeker's defenses) and adapt to attack patterns. Introduces a new combat mechanic or requires creative tactics.
5. **"The Precursor Gate"** — Reach the Convergence Point at the galactic core. Use the Convergence Key. A massive Precursor structure activates — the same one where Elenion fought the final battle. Inside, the ancient civilization's infrastructure is intact: not ruined, but maintained. Someone has been here, keeping it alive.
6. **"Echoes of the Builders"** — Inside the Convergence structure, find layered data logs. First: Precursor records predating even the Dominion — they built this place as a nexus of knowledge. Then: Dominion records from their era of rule. Finally: Elenion's own post-war logs, sealing the core and recording his hope that the integrated Dominion would help build a better galaxy. And buried deepest: recent logs from the Dominion Remnant — they've been here all along, studying the Precursor technology, preparing to reclaim what they believe is rightfully theirs.
7. **"The Heart's Choice"** — The Heart of the Galaxy (the Precursor power nexus) can do one of two things. The Oracle advocates **Elenion's Way**: reactivate the relay network and extend a diplomatic hand to the Dominion Remnant, offering integration again — the path of the book, the path of mercy. The Hermit advocates **The Final Seal**: weaponize the Heart and destroy the Dominion's infrastructure permanently, ensuring they can never rise again — but at the cost of losing irreplaceable Precursor knowledge. **Player choice moment** (affects Act IV).
8. **"Rally the Galaxy"** — Regardless of choice, you need all four races and five factions to contribute resources. Travel to faction leaders, convince them. Requires high reputation across multiple factions. This is the payoff for all the faction work the player has done — and it echoes Chapter 5 of the Saga, where Elenion convened the first coalition aboard the Star Seeker.

**Theme**: The endgame. The Umbral Dominion Remnant makes its move — emerging from the galactic core with ancient technologies and a fleet of Shade vessels. The entire galaxy mobilizes. The player's Act III choice shapes how this plays out: Elenion's Way offers a harder path to a richer peace; The Final Seal is a devastating but decisive military campaign.

**Story missions** (6 missions + repeatable war events):

2. **"Forging Alliances"** — Unite the fractured Kalin clans (invoke the legacy of Commander Raxus) and convince the Shadow Syndicate to fight openly. [ELENION'S WAY bonus]: Also recruit Dominion defectors — those who accepted Elenion's original peace and now oppose the Remnant.

## Epilogue / Post-Game

After completing Act IV, the player enters the post-game state:

- **Repeatable Dominion events** continue as endgame content (Shade patrols, sector defense)
- [ELENION'S WAY] **Galactic Council missions** — help the new Council resolve disputes, integrate Dominion defectors, and rebuild the relay network. Echoes Chapters 7-8 of the Saga.
- [FINAL SEAL] **Core Salvage missions** — explore the ruins of the galactic core for Precursor artifacts before they're lost forever
- **Elenion's Final Codex** teases a future expansion — coordinates beyond the Calvatian Galaxy
- **Mentor system**: Completed players can help new players through story missions for bonus rewards, earning the title "Navigator" (after Elenion's legacy)
- **New Game+** style prestige: optional reset with permanent stat bonuses carried over

## Implementation: Interstitial System

### Event Overlay Component

Reuse and extend the existing level-up celebration overlay pattern for all story moments:

**Types of interstitials**:
| Type | Duration | Trigger |
|------|----------|---------|
| Act Opening | 8s (click to dismiss) | First login after reaching act threshold |
| Act Completion | 10s (click to dismiss) | Completing final mission of an act |
| Major Reveal | 6s (click to dismiss) | Key lore moments mid-act |
| Player Choice | No auto-dismiss | Act III choice moment |

**Visual style**:

- Dark backdrop (rgba 0,0,0,0.95) with subtle star particle effect
- Monospace terminal-style text, typing animation
- Cyan/gold color scheme matching existing UI
- Act number in large display
- Ambient sound cue (low hum for ominous, triumphant chord for completion)

### Toast Notification System

**Story nudge toasts** — shown when:

- Player has been online 30+ minutes without story progress
- Player is in a sector near a story objective
- Player reaches a level threshold for the next act but hasn't started it

**Toast format**:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ ◆ STORY                            │
│ The Oracle has a message for you... │
│                          [DISMISS]  │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Rules**:

- Max 1 story toast per 15 minutes
- Don't show during combat or active missions
- Dismiss on click or after 8 seconds
- Different urgency levels: ambient (blue), important (cyan), urgent (gold)

## Implementation: Story Mission System

### Database Schema

```sql
-- Story progress tracking
CREATE TABLE story_progress (
  player_id INTEGER PRIMARY KEY REFERENCES players(id),
  current_act INTEGER DEFAULT 0,        -- 0=prologue, 1-4=acts
  act_started_at TIMESTAMP,
  act_completed_at TIMESTAMP,
  act3_choice TEXT,                      -- 'seal' or 'fight'
  fragments_found INTEGER DEFAULT 0,    -- bitmask: muscarian=1, vedic=2, kalin=4, tarri=8
  harbinger_encounters INTEGER DEFAULT 0,
  story_complete BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE
);

-- Individual story missions
CREATE TABLE story_missions (
  id SERIAL PRIMARY KEY,
  act INTEGER NOT NULL,
  sequence INTEGER NOT NULL,           -- order within act
  mission_key TEXT UNIQUE NOT NULL,    -- e.g. 'act1_signal_static'
  title TEXT NOT NULL,
  description TEXT NOT NULL,
  objectives JSONB NOT NULL,           -- [{type, target, count, current}]
  rewards JSONB NOT NULL,              -- {credits, xp, items[], title}
  requirements JSONB,                  -- {level, factions: {name: tier}}
  npc_id TEXT,                         -- key NPC associated with mission
  next_mission_key TEXT                -- chain to next mission
);

-- Player story mission progress
CREATE TABLE player_story_missions (
  player_id INTEGER REFERENCES players(id),
  mission_key TEXT NOT NULL,
  status TEXT DEFAULT 'available',     -- available, active, complete, locked
  progress JSONB,                      -- objective progress
  started_at TIMESTAMP,
  completed_at TIMESTAMP,
  PRIMARY KEY (player_id, mission_key)
);
```

## Open Questions

1. ~~**Agaricalis Saga integration**~~ — **DONE**. All 8 chapters mapped to game acts. Book characters integrated as historical figures. Antagonist refined from "Void Harbingers" to "Umbral Dominion" per book canon.
2. **Multiplayer story coordination** — Should syndicate members share story progress? Can they do story missions together?
3. **Dominion Shade enemy design** — New enemy type needs combat balancing. Should they be PvE-only or could players encounter Dominion-controlled sectors in PvP? Their "adaptive energy dispersion" tech (from book: Caelum reverse-engineered their defenses) could be an interesting combat mechanic.
4. **Voice/sound** — Story interstitials would benefit from ambient audio. Budget for this?
5. **Branching consequences** — The Act III choice (Elenion's Way vs Final Seal) now diverges meaningfully: different Act IV missions, different war events, different endgame content, different titles/rewards. Elenion's Way is harder but richer (more missions, Dominion defector mechanics). Final Seal is more straightforward military campaign.
6. **Elenion's legacy as game mechanic** — The book's themes of diplomacy vs force could extend beyond the binary choice. Players who build high reputation across all factions could unlock "Elenion's Approach" options in regular missions — diplomatic solutions alongside combat ones.
7. **Interstitial usage beyond story** — Per Dan's request, the fullscreen overlay ("interstitial") pattern should be used heavily throughout the game: mission completion, major discoveries, first-time events, faction rank-ups. Not just for act transitions. See separate discussion on this.
